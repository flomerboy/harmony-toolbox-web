About

Harmony Toolbox is made by me, Ryan Mather. As a twenty-something in New York I felt overwhelmed with all the possibilities for how to spend time. Should I draw? paint? learn to code? I started making tools to help me prioritize ideas. But the decisions I made in these early versions never quite stuck. My goals would change, or I'd lose interest.

I had a eureka moment when I took a class at the School for Social Design, which taught me about personal values. I realized that personal values were the best tools to make these kinds of life decisions. I redesigned my tools to an early version of Harmony Toolbox, and I started feeling a lot better about my decisions, had a better time sticking to them, and feeling good about how I spent my time, starting new habits and ending the ones I didn't like.

I wrote about my process for The Creative Independent and over the months following, I received mail from people all over the world who were trying out the tools in the article. But there were a lot of gaps in the article that made it It struck me that there was a need for a product that supported. And that's how Harmony Toolbox was born!
